## üîë Key Terms

### 1. Cybersecurity  
The practice of protecting systems, networks, and data from attacks, damage, or unauthorized access.

### 2. Threat  
Anything that has the potential to cause harm to a system or data.

### 3. Vulnerability  
A weakness in a system that attackers can exploit.

### 4. Exploit  
A technique or tool that takes advantage of a vulnerability.

### 5. Malware  
Malicious software designed to damage, disrupt, or gain unauthorized access (e.g., viruses, worms, trojans).

### 6. Phishing  
A social engineering attack where someone tricks me into giving away personal information.

### 7. Attack Vector  
The way an attacker gets into a system (email, USB, weak password, etc.).

### 8. Encryption  
A process that turns readable data into unreadable code to protect it.

### 9. Firewall  
A security tool that filters traffic and blocks unauthorized access.

### 10. Authentication  
Proving who I am (passwords, biometrics, MFA).

### 11. Authorization  
What I am allowed to access after I authenticate.
